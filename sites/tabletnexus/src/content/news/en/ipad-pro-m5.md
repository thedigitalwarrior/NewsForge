The new **iPad Pro M5** arrives just over a year after the M4 generation, and buyers keep asking the same thing: is it worth upgrading? The short answer is "it depends on what you do with it", but the concrete numbers are worth a look.

## The chip

The architectural jump brings efficiency gains more than raw peak performance. On sustained workloads — video editing, exports, demanding games — the difference shows up mostly in temperature and in how steady performance stays over time.

## Display and charging

The panel stays in the same family, with improvements to peak HDR brightness. Faster charging is the change you notice most in day-to-day use.

## Is the upgrade worth it?

Coming from a model two or more generations old, yes. If you already have an M4, the gains are incremental and hard to justify.
